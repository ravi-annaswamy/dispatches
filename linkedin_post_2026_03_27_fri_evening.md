Tonight's AI feed, filtered for signal: the things worth thinking about past next week.

A Stanford/Berkeley/CMU/MSR paper (@ChenLingjiao, amplified by @jmzou1) found that in 21.8% of model-pair comparisons, the cheaper-looking API actually costs more in practice. Thinking tokens vary up to 9.7× across repeated runs of the same query. Sticker price is now nearly meaningless without per-query profiling.

@riyatyagi86 and @NeelNanda5 built 9 tasks that prove reading an AI's chain of thought doesn't give you interpretability. The model may be hiding its actual reasoning in the underlying computation — the scratchpad is not the reasoning. "It's surprisingly hard to tell if you have made progress on interpreting reasoning models."

A new Google DeepMind paper (shared by @omarsar0) argues the intelligence explosion won't be a single superintelligent mind — it will be collective. Every historical intelligence explosion (language, writing, science, industry) was social. AI reasoning models already form internal "societies of thought" with specialization and hierarchy. The interesting unit of analysis is not the model; it's the group.

@karpathy ran autoresearch for two days on a small model. It found ~20 improvements. Every single one transferred to a 2× larger model. No cherry-picking. @neural_avb flagged the attribution problem this creates: how do you cite an automated research system?

@DeryaTR_ announced he will no longer review scientific papers. AI does 95%+ of the review better than he can. Alongside @LocasaleLab's note that a paper took 3 years from submission to publication — peer review is simultaneously too slow and too manual to survive.

@akshay_pachaar clarified the mental model most Claude Code users are missing: "CLAUDE.md is a suggestion. Hooks are a guarantee." PreToolUse and PostToolUse hooks can block or reshape tool calls programmatically. Exit code 2 is the only one that actually stops execution.

@paul_conyngham — not a biologist — designed a custom mRNA vaccine for his dog's cancer using AI, documented the whole thing. @DeryaTR_ called it "one of the historical inception stories in the age of AI." The capability is real. The institutional implications for medicine are unresolved.

@VictorTaelin tried to use frontier models to implement a termination checker. The plans were bad. His confession: "my brain is spoiled and lazy from so much vibe coding." Two things simultaneously true: AI can't do hard formal verification yet, and extended reliance on AI may be degrading the practitioner's capacity to do it without AI.
