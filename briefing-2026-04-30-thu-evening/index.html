The "No Engineer" Era
1 item
One tweet today did the work of a Substack essay. It's worth its own topic because the framing will get reused.
Jonathan Ross argued that for 50 years, software engineering ran on "code rationing" — and the rationing produced a specific organizational role: "the No Engineer," who said no to scope, no to bandwidth, no to the next idea, because writing code was expensive.
Ross's claim is that with code now cheap, the No Engineer's organizational function evaporates — the prioritization meeting, the RFC queue, the roadmap negotiation are all artifacts of a constraint that no longer binds. The implication is that the org chart, not the IDE, is the next thing to be rewritten.
