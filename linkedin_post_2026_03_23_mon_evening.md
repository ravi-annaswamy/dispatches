Something shifted today — not in any one announcement, but in the pattern.

Claude launched computer use for Mac. Not "chat with an AI." Not "ask it a question." Claude now physically clicks, scrolls, types, and navigates your desktop. It opens your apps, fills your spreadsheets, and operates your browser like a colleague sitting at your desk. Within hours, "Claude Cowork" was trending.

Meanwhile, Sahil Lavingia (Gumroad) turned his entire book The Minimalist Entrepreneur into 9 executable Claude Code skills. Instead of reading a chapter on pricing, you type /pricing and get an interactive analysis for your specific product. Books as code. Knowledge as workflow.

On the research side, Jeff Clune's team dropped HyperAgents — a system that doesn't just improve at tasks, but improves how it improves. The meta-learning recursion here is exactly the kind of advance that sounds theoretical until it starts outperforming fixed architectures on everything from coding to Olympiad math.

And perhaps the loudest signal: Jensen Huang told Lex Fridman, point-blank, that he believes we've achieved AGI. On the same day, someone ran a 35-billion-parameter model on an iPhone at 13 tokens per second. The frontier isn't just in the cloud anymore — it's in your pocket.

The thread connecting all of this isn't any single product launch. It's the moment AI stops being something you visit in a browser tab and becomes something that lives with you — on your desktop, in your phone, encoded in the books you read.

The age of AI-as-destination is ending. The age of AI-as-environment is beginning.
